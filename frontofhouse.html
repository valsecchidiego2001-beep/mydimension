Front of House

WHERE ARE WE

The Front of House represents the meeting point between organization and audience — between eyes and emotions, between the ideas of the producer and the sensations of the spectator. But it is more than that: the Front of House acts as a catalyst for human processes, encouraging cognitive engagement and supporting the development of ideas, concepts, and emotions. This space aims to play exactly that role. To act as a Front of House between readers — fostering the growth and sharing of meaningful ideas, and serving as a place where they can converge. This space is dedicated to everyone who has something to say, something to show, or something to persuade others of. It doesn’t matter what it is — if it sparks an emotion, you’re in the right place.

EPICENTRO

The electronic music landscape has, for many years, been in constant search of new ways to leave a lasting mark on the audience. Beyond sound itself, the live experience has become a crucial element in shaping how music is perceived and remembered. Among the many tools available to creators and producers, the stage plays a central role. Dimensions, scenography, colors, LED walls, technical solutions, positioning, and orientation are just some of the elements that define a stage and make it unique. Each design choice contributes to shaping the visual and emotional language of a performance, determining which elements of the show stand out and how the audience interacts with them.

When discussing prominence within a performance, an important question naturally arises: who is the true protagonist of a show? Is it the spectator, whose emotions and reactions give meaning to the experience? Is it the artist, the creative force that drives the performance? Or is it the art itself, the expression that connects both sides? Perhaps the true answer lies in the balance between all three. From this perspective, the world of production constantly explores ways to bring these elements together into a single core — a shared center, an epicenter of the show where art, artist, and audience coexist within the same experiential dimension. This vision has led to the development of 360° stages, designed to dissolve the traditional front-facing hierarchy between performer and spectator. By placing the artist at the center and surrounding them with the audience, these stages create a circular structure where the three fundamental elements of the show — art, artist, and spectator — become part of the same spatial and emotional system.

“Epicentro” mbraces this philosophy and transforms it into its essential concept. It proposes a specific and minimalist stage design that does not seek to dominate the scene, but rather to host and support the experience. Instead of drawing attention to itself, the structure quietly frames the moment, allowing the true protagonists of the show — art, artist, and audience — to emerge and interact naturally.
